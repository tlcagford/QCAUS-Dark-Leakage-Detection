# Milestone A — Tabletop proof-of-principle: single-photon -> dark-excitation conversion and readout

## Goal
Demonstrate a measurable signature of coupling between an electromagnetic mode (photon) and a dark-sector
excitation in a controlled, resonant environment. Provide controlled null tests.

## Core approach
- High-Q cavity resonator (microwave or optical depending on target m).
- Use magnetic field for axion-like conversion if needed.
- Quantum-limited readout (JPAs/SQUIDs for microwave; SNSPD/TES for optical).
- Single-photon or heralded source for injection, and rigorous control runs.

## Apparatus (example)
- High-Q cavity (Q >= 1e4), cryostat (4K/dilution as necessary), superconducting magnet (1–10 T).
- JPA/SQUID amplification chain OR SNSPD/TES detector chain.
- Laser/attenuated source or SPDC heralded source.
- DAQ, isolation, shielding.

## Measurements & analysis
- Sweep frequency, B-field, and presence/absence of injected photons.
- Control runs (detector-only, detuned cavity).
- Statistical detection criteria: 5σ peak after trials-correction or Bayesian odds ratio > 100.

## Budget & timeline (rough)
- Hardware: $0.5M–$3M. Personnel: 2–3 FTE for 12–24 months to first sensitivity.
